Project: Making User Science Journal Accessible

Posted by Simone Student on May 12, 2019

Rounding out on the topic of accessibility, this week we used the WAVE and Lighthouse accessibility tools to analyze our User Science Journal webpages. I focused on three pages: my Index, my About, and my Blog Post 5. Knowing the pages were mainly text with few images and external links, the results from the accessibility tools did not surprise me. Both tools found similar problems with each page.

Summary of Results and Fixes

The Lighthouse tool gave the Index page the lowest Accessibility rating of 78, mainly due to the font color contrasts not meeting standards. The tool also found dead links on the footer of the page. The WAVE accessibility tool found the same issues with the contrast colors on the Index page as Lighthouse. It also indicated structural elements such as headings were not clear and to ensure that the texts on the page were marked as headings were indeed headings.

I changed my font colors to black to increase the contrast. I also changed the Headings structure to make them more uniformed. Then I removed the undefined social media links on the footer of the page.

Second lowest on the Lighthouse tool rating was Blog Post 5 page, it received a 81, again mainly due to the font color contrasts not meeting standards and links on the footer that were undefined. The WAVE accessibility tool found the same issues with the contrast colors, it also cautioned about the links to PDF documents. Any links to external documents should also meet accessibility standards.

Again, I changed my font colors to increase the contrast and removed the undefined social media links on the footer of the page. I also added "alt" tags to the images.

The About page returned the least problems with the Lighthouse tool rating of 87. Both the Lighthouse and WAVE tools found errors with the undefined links in the footer of the page. It is surprising that a page with as little content as the About page can still return accessibility problems.

The fix for this page was simply to remove the undefined links on the footer of ther page.

Conclusion

In exploring the topic of accessibility, I've become keenly aware of the the work involved in making sure a page meets its standards. However, it has been proven crucial that we need to be attuned to it and build our websites with accessibility high on our priorities list from the beginning.
