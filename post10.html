It’s the 10th week of this User Science Journal!

Whoa Nelly! Time for a summarization and reflection of the last few weeks of testing and data analytics. Google Analytics(GA) has been the tool I used to collect data, set goals, and set experiments to drive visitors to complete the goals set for the webpage. I was astonished at the ease of integrating such a tool on the webpage. You can read the about how easy it was on my Blog Post 7. The inital gathering of the traffic data had a couple of surprises, one data showed the Index page was not the most viewed page and the other showing the source being from Direct rather than Social. I set a couple of goals for the Journal, which I discussed in detail on my Blog Post 8. The first goal was directing users to click on my Blog Psot 7 and the second was to have visitors click on the Subscribe! button on the Index page.

Content Experiment

Last week, I conducted an A/B testing experiment on the website to gauge how well a slight change will affect the coversion rate of a goal. My set goal was to drive visitors to click on the blue Subscribe! button on the Index page. I made a slight change to the location of the button by moving it to the from the left side of the screen to the right side.
