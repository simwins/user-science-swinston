Project: Usability Testing

Posted by Simone Student on April 21, 2019

This week I’ll share my findings from a usability test of a newly designed website for a local sushi restaurant, Sushi Maki. I chose this site because this business is in our neighborhood and we often visit it.

Test Summary

Purpose: To test for ease of use, clarity of offerings, and site’s purpose.

Testers: 14 year old girl, 8 year old boy, and middle-aged man.

Method: Users on laptops, using Chrome browser. I used a Google form to collate the data.

Results Summary

After each tester launched the homepage of the website, I followed a simple test script. All three testers were struck with the clean and uncluttered appearance of the page. It was clear to them the site was for a restaurant. All testers reported that it was clear what they can do from the homepage.

Their first click was to open the menu. One tester reported that the menu page was “not helpful” because it lacked pictures of the food, while another indicated that it was fine and gave it an element of surprise. All three testers agreed that the actual food often do not look like their pictures on the website. It was more important that the food ingredients were listed on the menu item than high-resolution pictures.

When prompted with the task of searching for a food item, all three users reported that it was easy to use. One tester indicated that the search engine did not account for misspellings.

When asked whether the site was clear on how you can order items, one tester answered yes, one tester said no, and tester answered “maybe.”

The last question for testers was in regards to the listed items on the menu banner on the home page. All three answered that it was “fine” and not changes were needed. One tester added more pictures of the food would make the site more attractive.

Next Step Suggestions

This site overall is usable. An enhancement could be making the search feature account for misspelled words. Also because it was not immediately clear to testers if they could use the site to order food online. It would be helpful if the site was more explicit that you can only call in your order. Furthermore, I would suggest they use their “Contact” form to direct people on how they could order through the website.
